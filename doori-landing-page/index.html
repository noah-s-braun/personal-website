A marketing site for an innovative grill

The Doori is a backyard grill optimized for skewer cooking foods to charred, flavorful perfection. To influence readers to back the project on Kickstarter, I designed a landing page that explained the Doori’s features and tied them to relatable benefits.

1Combining copywriting, marketing, & user experience

My first step in a web project is gathering and organizing the necessary content. I pulled information from the project’s Kickstarter, simplified the messaging, and structured the information: starting with the main value proposition, followed by an introduction of the product, then feature-benefit pairs, and finally a call-to-action urging the reader to back the project. I included social proof at the end to drive conversions.

2Wireframing the site

Organizing the content is essentially a verbal wireframe, which makes creating a visual one easy. I created simple components and text styles in Figma and laid out the page in a clear structure. My web development experience gave me crucial insight into making practical layouts.

3Introducing aesthetics and style—interface design

Once I had a solid foundation, I moved on to the colors and typefaces. I chose Montserrat and Bitter from Google fonts for the typography. I kept the design simple, with lots of white space to indicate a premium feel.
