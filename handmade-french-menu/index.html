Handmade French Menu

Light, subdued typography and a handmade aesthetic

La Petite France brings the flavors of Southern France all the way to Southern Missouri. Though their food is authentic, they wanted to avoid typical French stereotypes in their design. My job was to layout their menu with a fresh aesthetic that said—no, whispered—“France.”

1Finding inspiration

I looked beyond the average American’s first ideas of France during the research phase. After some digging, I found Menton, a beautiful city on the French Rivera. Its pastel buildings and lush greenery perfectly matched my vision for the menu, so I drew inspiration from its architecture and vibe throughout the project.

2Carefully selecting typography

I settled on typefaces that balance hierarchical clarity with aesthetics, emulating Menton’s cheerful feel. I also incorporated my own brush lettering as a personalized touch.

3AI illustrations

Maintaining the hand-done look of the menu means realistic food photography was out of the picture. Instead, I experimented with Photoshop’s Generative Fill to craft watercolor illustrations for each page.

The result of my efforts was a playful, colorful menu that invites patrons to relax, imagine the sea breeze, and enjoy fresh, delicious French cuisine.
